## Extracted Workflow

1. Wait until markers are close to finished before starting the release workflow.
2. Review late-submission indicators in the assessment first.
3. The recording says ordinary extensions are often already reflected in the system, but special cases still need checking.
4. Note and comment on students whose late penalty or zero result needs an explanation.
5. For team assignments, verify how extension handling affected the whole team before penalising individual teammates.
6. When releasing:
   select all students only if the full cohort is ready
   otherwise unselect the exceptions first
   then move the selected students through the release workflow state
7. After release, put the student-facing penalty explanation in comments and make the final numeric change in the gradebook/final grade area.
8. Distinguish:
   no submission
   wrong file or incomplete file
   notebook/file mismatch or similar submission problem
9. Turnitin workflow from the recording:
   markers do the first pass
   compare against the cohort baseline for that assignment type
   do not overreact to routine percentages
   investigate high outliers or obviously suspicious cases
10. If an integrity issue is suspected, escalate it formally and do not tell the student that misconduct is already confirmed.

## Important Notes From The Recording

- The guide stresses that comments are important so students understand why the visible final mark changed.
- The guide treats Turnitin review as an academic-integrity screening step, not a simple percentage threshold.
- The recording mentions reminding students clearly in announcements about penalties and submission expectations.
