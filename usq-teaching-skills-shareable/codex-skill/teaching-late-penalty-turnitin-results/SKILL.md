# Teaching Late Penalty Turnitin Results

## Overview

Handle late penalties, Turnitin review, and results release as one controlled workflow. The recorded guide is: wait until marking is substantially complete, review the built-in late indicators and extensions, release only the students who are ready, then use comments and the gradebook for final penalty communication, with Turnitin outliers escalated separately from ordinary marking.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the release window and policy inputs.
   Collect the due date, intended release date, approved extensions, late-penalty rule, team-assignment behavior, and whether this is a dry run or a live release.
   The recording suggests keeping enough buffer after the due date for markers to finish and for late issues to be checked before release.

2. Review the reference guide.
   Read [references/panopto-guide.md](references/panopto-guide.md).
   The reference includes workflow notes extracted from the actual recording.

3. Review late-submission indicators and extensions first.
   Check the assessment view for late indicators, extension handling, and special cases.
   The recording says many ordinary extensions are already accounted for by the system, but special cases still need notes.
   For team assignments, verify how the extension affected the team before penalising teammates.

4. Make the penalty trail explicit.
   Where the final visible mark will differ from the raw marking view, leave clear student comments explaining the late penalty or zero outcome.
   If a student is more than the allowed lateness window under the course rule, record that outcome clearly rather than silently changing the grade.

5. Release only the students who are ready.
   Select all students only after confirming the full cohort is ready, or unselect the exceptions before release.
   The recording shows releasing through the marking workflow state after selecting the intended students.

6. After release, use the gradebook for final grade adjustments.
   The recording says that once results are released, late-penalty adjustments should be reflected via comments plus the gradebook/final grade area, not by trying to rework the already-released marking grid as if it were the final student-facing grade.

7. Distinguish missing-submission cases.
   Separate:
   no submission
   wrong file or incomplete file
   notebook/file mismatch or other special submission issues
   Handle these via the course rule and documented comments.

8. Review Turnitin as an exception workflow, not a raw threshold rule.
   The recording says markers do the first-pass Turnitin check.
   Compare an individual result against the cohort baseline for that assignment type.
   High percentages alone are not enough; outliers or obvious suspicious cases need closer review.

9. Escalate integrity concerns without issuing your own verdict.
   If a case looks like academic misconduct, escalate it through the formal academic-integrity path.
   Do not tell the student that misconduct is confirmed before the formal process decides it.

10. Verify the released state.
   Re-open the activity and gradebook views to confirm the intended students were released and the final comments/grade adjustments are in the right place.

## Safety Checks

- Do not invent or approximate the penalty rule.
- Do not release results for students who still have unresolved submission, extension, or integrity issues.
- Do not turn a Turnitin percentage into a misconduct decision on your own.

## Execution Notes

- Use spreadsheet tooling when penalties need batch calculation or audit output.
- Use the Computer Use plugin for live browser actions in StudyDesk or related systems.
- If the user only wants preparation, stop at a reviewed checklist or ready-to-release summary.
