# Teaching Workflows

This file summarizes the four source-checked teaching workflows bundled in this pack.

## 1. Panopto Tutor Video Editing

Core flow:

1. Sign in to Panopto and open `My Folder`.
2. Create or confirm the course-specific folder.
3. Upload the recording and wait for editing controls to become available.
4. In `Edit > Settings`, rename if needed and untick the discussion/comment options.
5. Import automatic captions.
6. Add `Contents` markers so students can jump to sections.
7. Use `Cuts` only for genuinely wrong or redundant segments.
8. Apply edits and wait for processing.
9. In `Share`, switch access to the UniSQ organization scope used in the guide and copy the link.
10. In StudyDesk, add a `URL` activity, paste the Panopto link, move it into place, and make sure it is visible.

High-risk checkpoints:

- Do not widen sharing beyond the intended UniSQ scope without confirmation.
- Do not delete or replace the source recording without confirmation.
- Verify the final StudyDesk link from the student-facing side.

## 2. StudyDesk Setup For A Shorter Offering

Core flow:

1. Open the integrated shell and check what students can already see.
2. Clean up `Getting Started` and Orientation material first.
3. Hide assessments, practice exams, and other not-ready activities.
4. Check both the StudyDesk schedule and the course-administrator settings.
5. Make the week count and flexible-learning count match the institutional pattern.
6. Move or combine weekly content so the shell matches the shorter delivery.
7. Put course-introduction material in Orientation Week.
8. Recheck quiz due dates and copied dates after the restructure.
9. Release only the first needed materials, not the full shell at once.
10. Send an Orientation announcement and verify in student view.

High-risk checkpoints:

- Do not bulk-release all weeks in an active course without confirmation.
- Treat schedule settings and course-administrator settings as a paired check.
- Verify hidden items from student view before leaving the shell.

## 3. Finalise Grades And Examiner Return

Core flow:

1. Audit the StudyDesk gradebook first.
2. Confirm exam grades are correct, including multiple-attempt handling.
3. Export the grade report to Excel before transfer.
4. Check distribution, borderline results, and missing-assessment anomalies in the spreadsheet.
5. Preview and spot-check one or more students before transfer.
6. In the institutional system, handle manual result-code cases such as `IAS` and `IM` carefully.
7. Open `Examiner Return`, review everything, and approve class by class.
8. Complete the grade-distribution/significance section and save.

High-risk checkpoints:

- Do not approve anything while unresolved anomalies remain.
- Keep grade data confidential.
- If current institutional policy differs from the recording, current policy wins.

## 4. Late Penalties, Turnitin, And Result Release

Core flow:

1. Start only after marking is substantially complete.
2. Review late indicators, extensions, and special cases in the assessment view.
3. Leave clear comments where penalties or zeros affect the student-visible outcome.
4. Release only the students who are ready; exclude unresolved cases first.
5. After release, make final visible grade changes in the gradebook/final-grade area.
6. Distinguish no submission, wrong file, and other special submission cases.
7. Use Turnitin as a screening workflow, not a raw-threshold rule.
8. Escalate suspected misconduct formally instead of deciding it yourself.

High-risk checkpoints:

- Do not release unresolved integrity or extension cases.
- Do not rely on a Turnitin percentage alone.
- Do not tell a student misconduct is confirmed before the formal process decides it.
